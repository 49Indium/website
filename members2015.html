UQ Computing Society

Are you interested in computing? Do you want to learn new technologies, or hack around on fun projects? Are programming competitions your thing?

The UQ Computing Society is just the club for you!

UQCS is a great way to meet like-minded people in a fun enviornment. We have a wide range of activities planned for 2015, from programming and seminars to video games and competitions.

Hackathon

2015 is seeing the return of our flagship event, the UQCS Hackathon.

Teams will compete over several days to design and write an app, be it mobile, desktop or web based, that assists students or the wider community. This could be a new tool for students, or tapping into a public dataset and making it visible and accessible to more people.

The Hackathon will be held on the weekend of 8-9 August. Full details are available at the Hackathon website.

Tech Meetups

Held on a fortnightly basis, UQCS Tech Meetups are an opportunity for you to learn some new tricks and hear about various technologies from industry representatives, research staff and your peers.

Programming

Like programming? UQCS has a number of projects planned for 2015. We've open sourced everything we use, from the website, to our accounting. We will provide training for the languages and tools we use throughout the year, so this is a great way to learn something new and contribute to a group coding project.

Tech Talks / Seminars

UQCS holds regular seminars on a variety of technological topics. Topics planned for 2015 include: Functional programming, the Open Source movement, an introduction to LaTeX, vim, and the return of our 2014 Introduction to Version Control (Git/Github/Bitbucket) and Introduction to GNU/Linux talk.

These are just some of the topics currently planned, and naturally many more will be added throughout the year.

Social

Everyone likes to chill out, so UQCS has organised some events to help you do just that! In 2015, we have added new games nights, as well as drinks to help you wind down at the end of the semester.

Staff vs Students Video Games

Think you were hard done by with some marking? Looking for a bit of revenge on those who marked it? Well look no further!

UQCS runs regular games between students and ITEE Staff. Previous games have been Team Fortress 2 and Open Arena, but we are looking to expand this year and appeal to a wider variety of gamers. All you need is the game being played, a UQCS membership, and then showing up on time to the server. Details are given out in advance of each session.

Trivia Night

Held in conjunction with EBESS, our annual Trivia Night is again being held in Semester 2.

There will be several rounds, with questions spanning engineering, computing as well as popular culture. There will be some great prizes on offer, plus pizza and drinks for all who participate.

Board Games Nights

Video games are not the only type of games we like to play.

UQCS is launching Board Games Night, where you can sit down and challenge other students (and maybe a few staff...) to a game or three. Be it Settlers of Catan, Citadels or something else you would like to bring along, there will be plenty of games to choose from. And to make sure you don't go hungry, there will be food and drinks available throughout the night.

End of Semester Drinks

After all your hard work during the semester, you deserve a way to wind down. UQCS will be teaming up with UQAttic to hold two evenings of drinks and food, one for each semester.

More details will come later in the semester.

Competitions

UQCS will hold training for competitions throughout the year. These sessions will be held in conjunction with our Tech Meetups, lead by members of the UQCS Executive who have particpated in previous years.

Code Jam

Code Jam is an annual competition run by Google. Individuals compete across several rounds to reach the Grand Final, which will be held at Google's Los Angeles office in 2015.

Registration opens on March 10, with the Qualification Round is held on April 10. Code Jam training will start from Week 4 in Semester 1 for all interested.

ACM ICPC

The ACM International Collegiate Programming Contest is an annual competitive programming competitions that UQ has participated in over several years. Teams of three students compete in regional heats to try to progress to the World Finals. The Brisbane heats are held in Semester 2, giving plenty of time to practise.
